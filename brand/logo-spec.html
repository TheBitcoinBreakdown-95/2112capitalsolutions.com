Recipe applied (per ChatGPT's analysis): all numerals at 0.12em tracking, secondary line at 0.27em tracking, secondary line sized at ~14% of numeral height. Same composition across all eight cards so the only variable is the font.

Updated rankings (correcting two earlier misses):

★ Libre Bodoni — best free match to the AI image's mood. Has the long elegant curves and high contrast that Canela has, without Bodoni Moda's tighter apertures or GFS Didot's sharper rationalist serifs. This is the free pick.
GFS Didot — actual Didot port. Sharper, more rationalist than the AI gen — defensible if you want a more institutional read.
Old Standard TT — Didone-family, slightly heavier proportions.
Italiana — refined display Didone, all-caps only.
Bodoni Moda — Bodoni cut, tighter apertures than the AI gen.
Abril Fatface, Playfair, Marcellus, Cormorant — comparison endpoints to show why family matters. Skip.

Paid escalation (worth considering — the AI's character is genuinely Canela-esque):
• Canela Light — Commercial Type, ~$200–300/weight. Closest match.
• Tiempos Headline Light — Klim, ~$200/weight. Editorial luxury.
• Lyon Display Light — Commercial Type, ~$300+. Private-bank annual report.
• Noe Display Light — Schick Toikka, ~$80/weight. Sharper, more fashion-coded.
• Didot LT Std — Free with Adobe CC subscription, otherwise ~$50-100.

The honest call: Libre Bodoni gets us 80% of the way for $0. Canela Light gets us to 100% for ~$200. Both are valid. If 2112 is a Year-1 firm with a small client roll, ship with Libre Bodoni and budget Canela Light when client revenue clears the cost. If the brand is going to live for decades and the budget can absorb $200 today, just buy Canela Light now and skip the inevitable upgrade.
